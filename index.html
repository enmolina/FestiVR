About


Can virtual reality (VR) provide music lovers immersive access to their favorite festivals from the comfort of their home? FestiVR explores the extent to which virtual reality can connect geographically distant fans to music festivals in a more immersive way than live streams are able to do. The project allows users to experience festival footage that was streamed live, along side footage captured for VR. The VR component, which is accessed via an Oculus Rift, demonstrates the way that users might experience immersion in music festivals before, during and after a live performance. In addition, participants are able to explore festival grounds and even watch their favorite performances from the first row.

The average music festival ticket is more than $200 and prices are rising as festivals begin to incorporate gourmet food, art and interactive media. With the additional costs of travel and accommodation, it is increasingly difficult for fans to experience their favorite artists live, making VR an attractive alternative.
